Background

Born in Zagreb, Croatia, I'm 29 years old and my journey in software development has been a whirlwind of busy days, fun experiences, and fascinating moments. As a kid, I always liked to play with free random software apps that were included as free trials on monthly PC magazine subscription CDs. When I was 13 years old, I began teaching myself Photoshop and embarked on the exciting journey of creating custom free blog templates for a local blogging platform.

Later, I shifted to platforms like Joomla, WordPress, and PHPBB. I created web portal & forum where I shared small tutorials about web design, photoshop and all IT related topics. In our best days we had approx 500 unique daily users, hosting and domain cost was sponsored by local web hosting company, and website was featured in local computers magazine.

By 2012, things took a big turn. I decided to go to IT University to study web design and programming. To pay for my studies, I took on a full-time job in a massive brewery as a warehouse operator and forklift driver â€“ quite the balancing act!

In my second year at university, a remarkable programming professor altered the course of my journey. Their teaching method and engaging projects in the C language ignited my passion for programming, a fervor that continues to drive me. Recognizing my dedication, my professor's valuable recommendation subsequently secured my first full-time web development position at a renowned software agency.

After about three awesome years at the agency, I craved new experiences. So, I packed my bags and headed to Amsterdam to join a startup. Although my time there was short, I got a taste of the exciting startup scene. This decision marked the beginning of my journey into the world of entrepreneurship, as I started contracting after working for the agency full-time. Embracing the challenges and opportunities that came with freelancing, I honed my skills and expanded my network, all while maintaining the same passion that initially led me to the agency.

My next project was in Frankfurt, Germany, for one of the biggest banks in Europe, bringing with me the invaluable experience of leading a dynamic team in the creation of a foundational in-house library. This library served as the bedrock for all their cutting-edge web applications, streamlining development processes and ensuring consistency across the digital landscape.

After a fulfilling year in Frankfurt, my path guided me to Berlin, where I partnered with a dynamic medium-sized SaaS (software as a service) company for my next project. Their remarkable product had captivated the attention of some of the largest companies across Europe, who relied on it for their operations.

I returned to Zagreb as the pandemic started, seeking the comfort of my hometown. When the lockdown was lifted, a new opportunity emerged. As time has gone by, our co-working haven has become a place where our passions blend seamlessly with our daily work.
